# Kalsoni Website Redesign — v2 Update

**What this is:** a summary of the new version of the site mockup, built directly from your feedback. Every point you raised has been addressed. Below is a plain-language walkthrough of what changed, mapped back to your notes, plus a short list of what we'll need from you to finish.

---

## The short version

We took your feedback and the competitor research and rebuilt the mockup as **v2**. The catalog now reflects your real products (Biftu tunics, Wide Leg Pant, Joggers, Safiya & Mako Sport Hijabs and Shawls, plus bundles), and we added every feature you asked for — the Sport Hijabs & Shawls category is front and centre again, products show as colour swatches with multiple photos, bundles are real products, and there's a new lookbook and a product journal. **All of your recommendations are in.**

---

## What's new, point by point

### Homepage
- **Full-screen hero** — one big image fills the screen on arrival, just like the sites you liked.
- **Category tiles** (not text links) — **Tops, Bottoms, Sport Hijabs & Shawls, Bundles**.
- **Sport Hijabs & Shawls is back and prominent** — it has its own dedicated feature section with equal/greater weight than Tops and Bottoms, so it's never buried. This is the piece that sets Kalsoni apart, and the homepage now treats it that way.
- **Bundles featured up top** — bundle-first merchandising, the way Haya Active does it.
- **Shoppable Instagram section** — real-customer-style photos that link straight to the product being worn (tap to shop).
- **"Buying for a team or school?" link** near the footer.
- **Welcome pop-up** — asks how the shopper likes to move (running, yoga, etc.) then captures their email for a discount, the way Oiselle does.

### Product pages
- **Colours show as swatches on one page** — not five separate listings. Each style is one product with all its colours.
- **Multiple photos per colour** — every colour now has its own set of five shots (front, side, back, detail, flat-lay). Switching the colour swatch swaps the whole photo set.
- **"Complete the Look"** — every product suggests the matching pieces (e.g. a tunic suggests the Wide Leg Pant + a Sport Hijab), like Kith.
- **Size guide opens as a pop-up**, not a separate page.
- **Reviews are specific to each product** — each item shows reviews written about that exact piece.
- **Fabric is named and linked** (KalFlex / BreezeLuxe / SolShield), the way Qynda names its fabric tech.
- **Hover effect** — hovering a product photo flips from the model shot to a flat-lay, like Rains.

### Catalog & "making it feel full"
- **Colour packs / collection naming** — products are grouped under named collections ("The Biftu Collection," "The Performance Hijab Collection") and cards show the colour count ("5 Colors"), so a small catalog reads as a full, curated range.
- **Full size range** shown XS–5XL with an updated size chart.

### Bundles
- **Bundles are their own product type** (not just a discount code) — The Complete Set, Starter Kit, and Mix & Match 3 each have their own page that lists exactly what's included.

### Lookbook & Journal
- **Lookbook page** — a separate, editorial "drop" page (titled *Between Tides*) with a campaign story, distinct from the shop grid, like Merrachi. Gives that new-collection feeling even between core releases.
- **The Journal** — a new product blog for new and upcoming releases, colourway announcements, and behind-the-scenes stories, like the Kith product blog you liked.

### Wholesale / Bulk Orders (Schools & Teams)
- A dedicated **Bulk Orders** section was added to the Wholesale page covering who it's for (schools, teams, organizations), simple minimums (10+ units), and a proper **inquiry form** (organization name, contact, estimated quantity, products of interest) — not just an email link.
- Added to the **footer** and linked from the homepage.

### B Corp
- The **B Corp badge is back** in the homepage "stocked at" strip, sits in the **footer site-wide**, and there's a short **explainer** on the mission section describing what B Corp certification means.

### Other ideas
- **Affiliate program page** added (inspired by Karma) with how-it-works steps and a sign-up form.
