## 5. Strategies to Make the Catalog Feel Full

### A. Treat color as the primary merchandising lever
- ASRV groups single styles into named "Color Packs" (e.g. "Women's Color Pack") rather than listing each colorway as a separate product. Apply this to the Biftu Tunic: **"Biftu Collection — 5 Colors."**
- Each PDP should show all colorways as swatches on **one** product page, not as five separate listings.

### B. Build "Complete the Look" cross-sell
- KITH pairs every product page with a "Complete the Look" module showing coordinating pieces.
- For Kalsoni: every tunic should suggest the matching Wide Leg Pant or Joggers **+** a Sport Hijab/Shawl in a coordinating color.

### C. Name and brand each "collection" distinctly
- Lyra names collections ("Signature Knitwear," "ActiveCore Capsule") rather than listing raw product names.
- **Recommendation:** name the hijab/shawl line as **"The Performance Hijab Collection,"** and tunics as **"The Biftu Collection"** / **"Biftu Full Length"** — gives the impression of multiple curated drops even from a small SKU count.

### D. Multiply imagery per product (5–6 images minimum)
- Merrachi shows 5–6 images per product: editorial, studio front/back, and flat-lay front/back.
- For Kalsoni: each color of the Biftu Tunic should have a front, side, back, detail (zipper/pocket/wordmark), and flat-lay shot. This alone makes a 5-color product feel like ~25 distinct visual assets across the PLP.
- 🛠️ *Merchant note: currently working on creating new product images, TBD — can we have placeholder slots?*

### E. Bundle strategically
- Haya Active's "Mix & Match 3" bundle (from $89) is their featured best-seller — bundling, not individual items, anchors their homepage.
- Kalsoni already has bundle concepts in progress (The Complete Set, Starter Kit, Mix & Match 3) — make sure Shopline builds bundle PDPs as their own **product type**, not just a discount code.

### F. Editorial / lookbook page, separate from "Shop"
- Merrachi's homepage leads with a campaign hero + "Discover Lookbook" link to a styled editorial page, separate from the transactional shop grid.
- 🛠️ *Merchant note: working on a new collection and would love an area to give a look into the collection / idea behind it — to give the catalog a "drop" feeling even without new core styles.*

## 6. Recommendations

### 6.1 Homepage
- Full-screen hero image with one clear headline.
- Category tiles instead of text links — **Tops, Bottoms, Sport Hijabs & Shawls, Bundles**.
- **The Sport Hijabs & Shawls tile needs to come back.** It was on the live site, it's a real part of revenue, and it's one of the things that sets Kalsoni apart from other activewear brands. Give it equal or more visual weight than Tops/Bottoms — don't bury it.
  - 💡 *Merchant note: likes what [Knix](http://Knix) does with the cheetah collection section — this would fit well for the hijab.*
- Add a section pulling in real customer photos from Instagram, linked to the product they're wearing — UGC that's actually shoppable.
  - 🛠️ *Merchant note: something to work on and add in the future.*

### 6.2 Product Pages
- Colors need to show as **swatches on one product page**, not as separate listings per color.
- Each color needs at least **5 photos**: front, side, back, a detail shot, and a flat-lay.
  - 🛠️ *Merchant note: working on this, TBD — will have more images and content for the page.*
- Size guide should be a **popup/modal**, not a separate page click-through.
- Add a **"Complete the Look"** section suggesting the matching pant or hijab (or both).

## 7. Other Notes

> *Happy to hear other recommendations below, and how to implement them into the website.*

### 7.1 Wholesale / Bulk Orders (Schools & Teams)
- Add a **"Wholesale Inquiries"** or **"Bulk Orders"** link in the footer.
- Build a dedicated landing page covering:
  - Who it's for (schools, athletic teams, organizations).
  - Bulk pricing / MOQ info (even a simple "10+ units, contact us" is enough to start).
  - An inquiry form (org name, contact, estimated quantity, products of interest) instead of just a `mailto:` link.
- Add a small homepage text link near the footer ("Buying for a team or school? Let's talk.") — no full hero section needed yet.

### 7.2 B Corp Logo
- Restore the B Corp logo to the homepage "stocked at" strip, alongside REI, Target, Adidas, etc.
- Add it to the footer as a permanent trust-bar element, site-wide.
- Add a short explainer line on the About/mission page describing what B Corp certification means, since most shoppers won't know.
